See Beyond Emissions.

GreenSight is a sustainability intelligence platform designed to help organizations track emissions, assess ESG maturity, and surface operational risks through executive-ready dashboards and insights.

The current MVP provides deterministic analytics and reporting workflows that are structured for future AI-powered reasoning and automation.

About the Project

What GreenSight Is

GreenSight is a web-based sustainability analytics application that organizes operational resource data into clear emissions metrics, visual dashboards, ESG maturity scoring, and executive-ready summaries.

Why It Was Created

The Office does not collect sustainability data and also struggle to translate it into decision-grade insight. GreenSight was created to bridge the gap between raw operational inputs and strategic sustainability management.

The Problem It Solves

It reduces fragmentation in sustainability reporting by centralizing key indicators, highlighting risks, and making trends understandable across departments and time periods.

Target Users

GreenSight is built for executives, sustainability officers, operations managers, finance leaders, and cross-functional teams responsible for emissions performance and ESG readiness.

Purpose

GreenSight supports operational execution and strategic sustainability decision-making in one workflow.

Track Sustainability Metrics

Capture electricity, fuel, and paper usage data and convert it into standardized CO2e metrics.

Assess ESG Maturity

Use a transparent scoring framework to summarize sustainability operating discipline and trend stability.

Identify Emission Risks

Flag high-emission departments and detect concentration risk areas that require operational attention.

Support Cost Optimization

Highlight potential efficiency opportunities linked to energy, fuel, and resource usage patterns.

Enable Executive Decisions

Translate sustainability performance into concise insights suitable for leadership reviews and planning.

Key Features

Core capabilities available in the current GreenSight MVP and near-term product structure.

Sustainability Dashboard

Centralized KPI, charting, and department-level emissions visibility.

ESG Maturity Scoring Framework

Deterministic 0-100 score with levels, risk band, and pillar breakdown.

Trend analysis across months with composition and department breakdown charts.

Board-ready summary language for leadership communication and planning discussions.

How to Use GreenSight

Standard workflow for using the MVP to move from data input to executive-level insight.

  1. Step 1: Input sustainability data

    Enter CSV data or manual monthly records for electricity, fuel, and paper usage by department.

  2. Step 2: Review Dashboard metrics

    Open the dashboard to review total emissions, department contributions, and trend visualizations.

  3. Step 3: Analyze ESG Maturity Score

    Assess the ESG score, maturity level, risk band, and pillar breakdown to understand performance posture.

  4. Step 4: Review AI Insights

    Use the simulated insights page to review forecasts, anomalies, recommendations, and executive summary outputs.

  5. Step 5: Use results for decision-making

    Apply findings to prioritize operational improvements, budgeting, reporting, and leadership discussions.

ESG Scoring Methodology (Brief Overview)

GreenSight uses a simple, transparent ESG maturity framework designed for explainability and future expansion.

4-Pillar Framework

Energy efficiency, fuel impact, paper efficiency, and emission trend stability are scored as separate pillars.

0-100 Total Score

Each pillar contributes up to 25 points, resulting in a total ESG maturity score between 0 and 100.

Level 1-5 Maturity Model

The total score maps to maturity levels from Initial to Optimized for quick executive interpretation.
